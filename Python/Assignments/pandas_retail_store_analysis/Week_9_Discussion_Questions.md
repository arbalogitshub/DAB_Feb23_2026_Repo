<b>1) DateTime Benefits: How can extracting day of the week and month from dates help a retail business make better decisions? </b><br />

Extracting the day of the week and month from transaction dates allows a retail business to perform time-series analysis, such as visualizing monthly sales trends or comparing sales performance across different days of the week. 

For example:

Day of the week insights:
* If sales are consistently lower on Tuesdays, the business could run a midweek promotion, adjust staffing levels, or launch a loyalty reward to boost traffic.

Month-based insights: 
* Recognizing that sales peak in November but dip in February could guide inventory planning (stocking up before high months) and targeted marketing (discounts or clearance events in slow months).

These insights help optimize promotions, staffing, inventory management, and marketing spend turning raw date data into actionable business decisions.

Looking at indidual tansactions only shows insolated events, making difficult to indentify patterns or trends. By grouping data by categories, states, or time periods, we can summarize the data and reveal insights such as which region performs best, how sales change over time, or which category generates the most revenue. This makes it easier to understand the overall picture and supports better, data-driven business decisions.
